What Needs to Change

Four Workstreams

The nine meeting decisions group into four workstreams. These aren't separate tracks — they interleave across quarters.

Architecture

1. Platform Unification

Replace the Hub launcher grid with a role-aware sidebar/nav. One shell, one login, sections instead of apps. Each existing app's LoggedLayout converges to a shared navigation component. Cards go behind a flag until consolidation.

Touches: Hub, all app layouts, WorkOS feature flags, routing

Execution

2. Engagement Restructure

Rename "Playbook" → "Engagements." New top-level "Playbook" for winning moves + strategic narrative. Activities gain child tasks (parent/child hierarchy) with assignments to any user (consultants + clients), public comments and private notes (both threaded), date ranges replacing time buckets, and relationships standardizing on the Asiot template.

Touches: Workbench, Client Portal, activities table, activity_assignments, activity_comments, PlaybookTransitionProvider, winning_moves

Product

3. Studio Experience

Merge Prompts + Library into a unified prompt studio. Build the personal use case generator. Position Academy as enablement within the Studio, not a separate destination. This is the lock-in layer — personal data creates switching costs.

Touches: Prompts app, Library app, Academy app, new use case generator

Infrastructure

4. Learning Infrastructure

Content curation modeled on platforms like ProfAI — bidirectional integration with the Workbench Context research pipeline. Client context (industry, strategy, competitive landscape) feeds relevant learning resources and use cases into the Academy; employee-discovered use cases feed back into strategic context. On top of this, Academy's JSONB carousel becomes a real module system: learning paths, assessments, certificates, progress tracking, and xAPI export for enterprise LMS integration.

Touches: Academy app, new tables (learning_paths, modules, assessments), xAPI endpoint, Resend, Context research pipeline

Why This Order

Sequencing Logic

The ordering isn't arbitrary.

Q1 is structural

The platform shell, engagement restructure, and Studio core are foundational. Nothing in Q2 or Q3 works without them. Six weeks, mid-Feb through end of March. Biggest risk phase — significant architectural change to a ~140K-line codebase with 10 apps.

Q2 validates the business model

Guided refresh + sprint workflow is the critical test. If the platform replaces biweekly check-ins with quarterly sessions + on-demand sprints, consultant capacity doubles from ~4 to ~8 concurrent clients.

Lock-in before content

Prompt library and use case generator ship before learning paths. An employee with 20 saved prompts and 8 use cases won't leave the platform even if the Studio content is mid. The reverse isn't true.

Enterprise unlocks don't block sales

xAPI foundation in Q1, full endpoint in Q2. Enterprise sales conversations in H2 2026 can include LMS integration as a capability, not a roadmap item.

Q3 absorbs overflow

Cards consolidation, reporting, and scale polish. If Q1 and Q2 go well, Q3 is cohesion + scale. If they slip, Q3 is the buffer.

One developer reality

Israel is the sole developer. Each quarter delivers a coherent milestone. No parallel tracks that split focus. Work within each quarter is sequenced, not parallelized.

Open Items

1
Navigation design — sidebar vs. top nav, collapsed vs. expanded, mobile behavior. Drives the entire shell implementation.
2
Multi-app or single-app shell? — shared nav component across existing apps (incremental) or new unified app replacing Hub (cleaner, more work). Architecture decision needed.
3
Asiot relationship documentation — capture the canonical patterns before Q2 standardization work.
4
Content authoring investment — AI Foundations path needs 6–8 module outlines. ~40 hours co-founder commitment.
5
Pilot client selection — who gets the unified platform first? What's the tolerance for rough edges?
6
Pricing model — workspace per-user or included in base? Affects invite flow design.
7
Cards consolidation design — "inline tools in the workspace" is a concept, not a design. Needs exploration in Q2 to ship in Q3.
