Who Is This For

Three Users, One Platform

The Transformation OS serves different needs at different frequencies — but the transformation lead is the primary user whose experience determines success.

Primary

Transformation Lead

Mid-to-senior professional given the mandate to "make AI happen." New to the role, accountable but not always empowered, drowning in complexity, often lonely.

  • A system that makes them look competent
  • Keeps them organized across dozens of moving parts
  • Gives them ammunition for leadership conversations
  • Helps them grow into a demanding role
Secondary

Leadership Team

C-suite and senior management who sponsor the transformation. Don't use the platform daily — want confidence and clarity.

  • Visible progress, not just activity
  • Briefing they can absorb in 10 minutes
  • A clear story for their board
Tertiary

Tatoma Consultant

Our team — needs the platform to scale impact, stay informed, and deliver sharper sessions.

  • Serve more clients without more hours
  • Know what's happening between sessions
  • Arrive at quarterly sessions already briefed
Why This Matters

The Broken Model

We deliver a great kickoff. Then we go away for two weeks. What happens in those two weeks? Almost nothing.

The Current Failure Mode

The TL gets pulled into daily operations. The playbook sits untouched. Next check-in: 30 min figuring out where things stand, 30 min rebuilding momentum.

  • We're selling time, not outcomes
  • We can't scale — each client costs proportional hours
  • We can't prove value — no data trail
  • The client doesn't own it — we push, they comply

The Transformation OS Hypothesis

  • Keep the transformation visible and top-of-mind, even when we're not in the room
  • Create structured touchpoints (guided refresh) that replace ad-hoc check-ins
  • Surface problems before they become crises
  • Give the TL tools to do their job better independently
  • Build the TL's capabilities over time

Phase A+B are the validation

The Living Playbook (Phase A) is the foundation — nothing works without it. The Guided Refresh (Phase B) is the single most important capability. If it works, we've proven the platform can replace biweekly check-ins. That's the entire business model hypothesis.

What Could Go Wrong

Risks & Mitigations

The five risks that could derail the Transformation OS — and how to address them.

High

"They won't come back to the platform"

The existential risk. A beautiful platform nobody opens is a failed product.

Mitigation: The nudge system is not nice-to-have. Weekly digests and health alerts are the primary engagement driver. Budget significant effort for email quality and relevance.
Medium

"The content is mediocre"

Narratives, MiniMBA, and onboarding content set the tone for the entire experience.

Mitigation: Phase A content must be authored by co-founders, not delegated. AI-assisted drafting is fine for scale, but initial content needs to be genuinely good.
Medium

"Clients resist the shift from biweekly to quarterly"

Some clients may feel abandoned when check-in frequency drops.

Mitigation: Frame as upgrading, not reducing. Pilot with Acito first. Keep biweekly available as premium add-on if needed.
Medium

"We can't build it in 12 weeks"

Ambitious scope for one developer.

Mitigation: Phase A is non-negotiable. Phase B is must-have for validation. Phases C+D can slip to Q3 without killing the story. Each phase delivers standalone value.
Medium-Low

"Sprint briefs don't convert to revenue"

The sprint model is new and unproven.

Mitigation: Sprint workflow is Phase D (last). By then we'll have data on demand. If low, pivot the UX to make the need more visible.

What to Discuss

Six decisions that need co-founder alignment before we start building.

1

Phase B timing

The guided refresh is the most important feature for model validation. Should we start it in parallel with Phase A (weeks 2-3) rather than waiting until week 4?

2

Pilot client

Acito is the obvious candidate. Are they the right choice? What's the risk if the first version is rough?

3

Pricing transition

When do we start quoting new clients on the Transformation OS model (setup + subscription) vs. the current model?

4

Content investment

Onboarding and MiniMBA content needs to be genuinely good. Are co-founders willing to invest ~20 hours in initial content creation?

5

Learn & Create timing

The strategy session called it "the most urgent build." Should it run in parallel with Transformation OS, or sequenced? One developer — what's realistic?

6

Sprint pricing

How do we price Spikes (~2d), Sprints (~1w), and Specials? Fixed fee per type, or scoped per engagement?
