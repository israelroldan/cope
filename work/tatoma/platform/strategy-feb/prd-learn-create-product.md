## The One-Line Pitch

**Learn & Create is the AI adoption engine for the entire organization** — where every employee discovers what AI can do for *them*, builds the skills to use it, and has always-on tools to make it real.

---

## 1. Who Is This For?

### The Key Insight: Different Product, Different Scale

The Transformation OS serves 3-10 people per client. Learn & Create serves **everyone else** — potentially hundreds or thousands of employees. This isn't a feature addition to the Transformation OS. It's a fundamentally different product with different design constraints, different engagement patterns, and a different role in the business.

### Primary User: The Employee

An individual contributor or team lead somewhere in the organization. They heard about AI, maybe tried ChatGPT once, and fall into one of three mindsets:

**The Curious (40%)** — interested but don't know where to start. "I hear AI can help with my work, but I don't know how it applies to what I do."

**The Skeptic (35%)** — tried it, got mediocre results, wrote it off. "I asked ChatGPT to write an email and it sounded robotic. AI isn't for me."

**The Early Adopter (25%)** — already using AI tools but ad-hoc, no structure, no sharing. "I've got my own prompts in a notes app. Works for me."

**What they all share:** They need AI to solve *their* problems, not abstract corporate ones. The entry point is personal productivity, not transformation strategy.

### Secondary User: The Transformation Lead

Uses Learn & Create data to demonstrate organizational adoption to leadership. They need:

- **Adoption metrics** to justify the investment ("200 employees completed AI Foundations")
- **Department-level insights** ("Marketing is leading adoption, Operations needs attention")
- **Success stories** to amplify ("Here are the top use cases employees discovered")

### Tertiary User: HR / L&D

May eventually own the platform relationship from the client side. Cares about:

- **Compliance** — can they track who completed mandatory AI training?
- **LMS integration** — does it connect to their existing learning systems?
- **Certificates** — something tangible to show for the investment

## 2. The Problem We're Solving

### Why Corporate AI Training Fails

Most organizations approach AI adoption like this:

1. **Leadership announces** "We're going all-in on AI"
2. **A one-day workshop** happens — usually inspiring, occasionally hands-on
3. **Everyone goes back to their desks** and nothing changes
4. **Six months later:** "Why isn't anyone using AI?"

The fundamental problem: **training is an event, not a system.** There's no ongoing support, no personal relevance, no tools to practice with, and no way to track whether anyone actually changed their behavior.

### What Section (Prof AI) Got Right — and Where They Stop

Section pivoted their entire org to product-led AI training. They understand that:

- Training needs to be self-service and always-available
- Use case discovery must be personal, not generic
- Certificates and social proof matter
- Consumption pricing works (per-user tiers)

**But Section doesn't connect training to transformation.** Their product is standalone — great for AI literacy, but it doesn't feed into a strategic transformation OS. There's no link between "I discovered a use case" and "this feeds into the company's AI roadmap." That's Tatoma's edge: the two products share a brain.

### The Learn & Create Hypothesis

What if we built a platform where:

- Employees discover AI use cases **relevant to their actual daily work** (not generic "101 AI use cases" lists)
- They build prompt skills through **guided creation, not just reading** about it
- They have **always-on AI tools** (Cards) for common tasks, so the value is immediate
- Their individual discoveries **bubble up** to the organizational level, feeding the Transformation OS with real adoption data and use case pipelines
- The whole thing feels like a **consumer product** — not corporate training software

## 3. The User Journey

### Journey 1: From Invite to First Win (Week 1)

**Today:** Employee attends a half-day workshop. Gets inspired. Goes back to their desk. Opens ChatGPT. Types something. Gets mediocre result. Never comes back.

**With Learn & Create:**

| Day | What happens | Employee feels |
|-----|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | Gets invite email from their transformation lead. Clean branded sign-up. | "Oh, the company is serious about this." |
| 1 | Quick department selection + role description during onboarding | "It knows what I do." |
| 2 | Starts "AI Foundations" path. Module 1: What AI can (and can't) do. | "Ok, this is actually clear." |
| 3 | Module 2: Your first useful prompt. Hands-on exercise: rewrite a real email. | "Wait — that actually worked." |
| 5 | Completes 3 modules. Tries a Card (Email Writer). Uses it for a real task. | "I just saved 15 minutes." |
| 7 | Gets a nudge: "Ready to find AI use cases in your daily work?" Opens the generator. | "It found 3 things I can try this week." |

**Key moment:** The employee uses an AI tool for a real work task and thinks "that was faster." That's the hook.

### Journey 2: Building Habits (Month 1-3)

| Week | What happens | Value |
|------|-------------|-------|
| 2-3 | Completes AI Foundations path. Earns certificate. | Social proof — posts on LinkedIn or shares with team |
| 4 | Uses prompt generator to create a weekly report template | Saves 30 min/week, every week |
| 5-6 | Discovers 2 more personal use cases via the generator | Growing personal AI toolkit |
| 8 | Attends "AI Hands-on" training session. Platform has pre-work + follow-up. | Live session with immediate practice |
| 10 | Starts "AI for Your Role" path (department-specific) | Deeper, role-relevant skills |
| 12 | Has 5 saved prompts, 4 use cases tried, 2 Cards in favorites | AI is part of daily workflow |

**Key moment:** The employee opens Learn & Create before opening ChatGPT, because their curated tools and prompts are better than starting from scratch.

### Journey 3: The Organizational Flywheel

This is where Learn & Create becomes more than individual training:

- **200 employees complete AI Foundations** → TL shows leadership a 40% completion rate in quarter one
- **The top 15 employee-discovered use cases** bubble up to the Transformation OS → consultant reviews and promotes 5 to the playbook
- **Department adoption data** reveals Marketing at 80% engagement, Operations at 15% → signals where to deploy next sprint
- **At renewal:** "Your employees discovered 47 AI use cases, saved an estimated 200 hours/month, and 85% of trained employees are still active on the platform. Here's your AI literacy report."

**Key moment:** A use case discovered by an employee in Learn & Create gets promoted to a formal activity in the Transformation OS. The bottom-up meets top-down.

## 4. What We're Building (Product Lens)

### The Five Capabilities

| Capability | User Need | How We Deliver | Engagement Pattern |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Academy + Certificates** | "How do I even start with AI?" | Structured learning paths, hands-on exercises, branded certs | First 2-4 weeks (intense), then periodic |
| **Personal Use Case Generator** | "What can AI do for MY job?" | Conversational AI flow → personalized suggestions | Monthly check-ins (discover new ones) |
| **Prompt Generator / Refiner** | "How do I write prompts that work?" | Step-by-step builder + paste-and-improve tool | Weekly (build library over time) |
| **Cards / Agents** | "Give me a tool I can use right now" | Pre-built AI assistants for common tasks | Daily (the always-on layer) |
| **Training Sessions** | "I want hands-on practice with others" | Session catalog, materials hub, follow-up | Quarterly (event-driven) |

### The Engagement Funnel

```
AWARENESS ─── Employee gets invited ──────────────── 100% invited
     │
     ▼
ACTIVATION ── Signs up, starts first module ────────── 60% register
     │
     ▼
FIRST WIN ─── Uses a Card or prompt for real work ──── 40% first win
     │
     ▼
HABIT ─────── Returns weekly (prompts, cards) ─────── 25% weekly active
     │
     ▼
ADVOCACY ──── Shares use cases, earns certs ────────── 15% advocates
     │
     ▼
FLYWHEEL ──── Use cases feed Transformation OS ─────── org-level impact
```

The product must be designed to move people DOWN this funnel. Each capability maps to a funnel stage:

- **Academy** = Activation + First Win
- **Cards** = First Win + Habit (the daily hook)
- **Prompt Generator** = Habit (the weekly tool)
- **Use Case Generator** = Advocacy + Flywheel
- **Training Sessions** = Habit reinforcement (the social layer)

## 5. Design Principles

### 1. Personal before organizational

The employee doesn't care about "AI transformation." They care about "can I leave work 30 minutes earlier?" Every feature should start from personal value and only later connect to the org level.

### 2. Show, don't teach

The fastest path to adoption isn't reading about AI — it's doing something with AI and seeing the result. Every learning module must include a hands-on exercise. Cards provide instant value without any learning.

### 3. Lower the floor, raise the ceiling

A complete beginner should feel confident starting (low floor). An advanced user should find value in the prompt refiner and custom use cases (high ceiling). Don't make the beginner path feel childish or the advanced path feel inaccessible.

### 4. Make it feel like a product, not training software

No "mandatory training" vibes. No corporate language. Clean design, fast interactions, mobile-friendly (even if not native). Think Duolingo, not Cornerstone.

### 5. Everything feeds up

Individual discoveries should aggregate into organizational intelligence. Use case counts, skill levels, adoption metrics, popular prompts — all visible to transformation leads without employees doing extra work.

## 6. Prioritization

### What Matters Most for Launch

The first version needs to nail **three things:**

**1. The Academy must feel complete and polished.**
If the first learning path ("AI Foundations") feels like a corporate slide deck, we've lost. It needs to be conversational, practical, and end with a genuine "aha" moment. This is content quality, not feature quantity.

**2. Cards must provide instant value.**
Cards are the "try before you learn" entry point. An employee who's skeptical about training will try a card. If the Email Writer or Meeting Prep card saves them time, they'll explore more. Cards first, academy second — even though the build order may differ.

**3. The use case generator must feel personal.**
"What can AI do for HR?" is interesting. "What can AI do for *you*, a benefits coordinator who spends 3 hours a week on enrollment questions?" is compelling. The AI-assisted scoping must make it personal.

### What Can Wait

- **LMS integration** — nice for enterprise sales, not needed for product validation
- **Cross-client content sharing** — valuable at scale, premature with 3 clients
- **Gamification** — can drive engagement, but substance matters more first
- **Department-specific learning paths** — Phase D; get the universal path right first

## 7. What Changes for the Business

### The Retention Engine

Learn & Create solves the single biggest risk to Tatoma's recurring revenue: **year-two churn.**

| Scenario | Without Learn & Create | With Learn & Create |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Year-two renewal** | "What did we get?" — hard to prove value beyond consulting hours | "200 employees trained, 47 use cases discovered, AI tools used daily" |
| **Stakeholder buy-in** | Only the TL and leadership see the platform | Every employee who uses a Card or earns a cert is a platform advocate |
| **Switching cost** | Low — only 3-10 users affected | High — hundreds of employees have personal libraries, prompts, certs |
| **Expansion revenue** | Upsell requires new consulting engagement | Consumption-based growth: add more users, more departments, more training |

### Pricing Implications

The strategy session discussed consumption-based pricing with user tiers:

| Tier | Users included | Above base |
|------|---------------|------------|
| **Kickstarter** | Up to 100 | Per-user add-on |
| **Accelerator** | Up to 250 | Per-user add-on |
| **Leader** | Up to 500 | Per-user add-on |

**Key question for tomorrow:** Is Learn & Create included in the base subscription or priced separately? The argument for including it: it drives stickiness that protects the Transformation OS subscription. The argument for pricing separately: it has clear standalone value and usage-based costs (AI inference).

### Content as Competitive Moat

Every learning path, prompt template, and department use case template Tatoma creates is reusable across all clients. The cost of content creation is amortized over the entire client base. Section figured this out — and their content library is their moat. Tatoma needs to build the same muscle, but connected to the transformation layer.

## 9. Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: "Nobody completes the training"
**Severity:** High — this is the engagement cliff.
**Why it happens:** Corporate training has a ~15% completion rate industry-wide. Employees start, get bored, stop.
**Mitigation:** Make the first 3 modules irresistible. End each one with a hands-on win. Keep modules short (10-15 min). Use Cards as the "instant gratification" alternative to training — even non-completers get value.

### Risk 2: "The content isn't good enough"
**Severity:** High — we have one shot at first impressions.
**Why it happens:** AI training content is either too basic ("what is machine learning?") or too technical ("here's how transformers work"). Neither helps an employee do their job better.
**Mitigation:** Every module must answer "what can I do differently tomorrow?" Co-founders write the first path. Test with real employees at a pilot client before broader launch.

### Risk 3: "It feels like corporate training software"
**Severity:** Medium — kills engagement before it starts.
**Why it happens:** LMS-style interfaces, mandatory tone, dry content, slow loading.
**Mitigation:** Design for consumer-grade UX. No "required training" language. Fast, clean, Tatoma-branded. Think Duolingo's energy, not Moodle's interface.

### Risk 4: "AI inference costs explode with 1000 users"
**Severity:** Medium — real financial risk at scale.
**Why it happens:** Use case generator, prompt refiner, and Cards all call AI APIs. At 100x the user base of Transformation OS, costs scale fast.
**Mitigation:** Use Groq (fastest, cheapest inference). Rate-limit AI features per user (e.g., 20 use case scopes/month, 50 prompt refinements/month). Cache common prompt templates. Monitor cost per client weekly.

### Risk 5: "One developer can't build both products in parallel"
**Severity:** High — the most practical risk.
**Why it happens:** Transformation OS alone is a 12-week build. Learn & Create is another 12 weeks.
**Mitigation:** Sequence, don't parallelize. Transformation OS first (validates the business model), Learn & Create second (Q3). OR: build Learn & Create Phase A (Academy) in parallel with Transformation OS Phase C-D, since they're independent codebases.

## 10. What to Discuss Tomorrow

### Decision 1: Sequencing
Transformation OS first, then Learn & Create? Or parallel tracks? One developer makes parallel risky. But the strategy session called L&C "the most urgent build." What's the real priority?

### Decision 2: Minimum viable Academy
How many learning modules do we need for launch? 6-8 for "AI Foundations" is the plan — but writing 8 quality modules is 40+ hours of content work. Who does this? Can we launch with 4 and expand?

### Decision 3: Cards as the entry point
Should we make Cards available before the Academy is ready? Cards provide instant value and don't require content creation. They could be the "early access" version of Learn & Create while the Academy is built.

### Decision 4: Pricing model
Included in base subscription or priced separately? Consumption-based (per user above a threshold) or flat? This affects how we present it to clients and how we design the invite flow.

### Decision 5: Content authoring investment
The co-founders need to invest real time in content creation for L&C to work. Is there commitment for ~40 hours of initial content work (outlines + review of AI-expanded drafts) across the three of you?

### Decision 6: Pilot scope
When we pilot L&C with a client, how many employees do we target? All of them (risky at scale)? A single department (safer but less data)? A volunteer group (self-selecting bias)?
