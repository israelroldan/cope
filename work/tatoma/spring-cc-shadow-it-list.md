## Monitored Shadow IT Categories

1. **Micro-SaaS services** for online conversion and processing of files
2. **Web-based generative AI services** and other AI processing platforms without a company license
3. **Browser extensions and plugins** installed without approval from IT
4. **High-performance web-based file-sharing services** (e.g., WeTransfer-like solutions)
5. **Project, ideation, and collaboration platforms** (e.g., Miro, Pinterest, etc.) used without official approval
6. **Private cloud storage services** used for company-related data (e.g., Google Drive, personal OneDrive, Dropbox)
7. **Unofficial AI services or personal AI licenses** used for work tasks
8. **Switching from company-managed iCloud to personal iCloud** to enable additional iCloud services
9. **Mobile apps on personal devices** where company data can be stored, cached, or transferred outside the company's control
