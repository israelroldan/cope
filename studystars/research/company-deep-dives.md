## Early Steps Academy

### Value Proposition

Early Steps Academy positions itself as an after-school program that bridges the gap between traditional schooling and the real-world skills children need for the future. It claims to solve the problem of irrelevant, rote learning by teaching "1000+ essential subjects not taught in school" through case studies.

The program promises outcomes like:
- Significantly improved critical thinking, communication skills, and confidence
- Better grades and future success
- "10× Confidence" for children
- College and career readiness

The case-study method, adapted from Harvard's approach, is touted as the secret sauce that gives children a "tremendous edge" in life. Marketing messaging plays on parents' aspirations and fears, stressing that early exposure will help kids get into the "best colleges and grabbing the best jobs."

**Overall positioning:** Early Steps sells peace of mind that one's child will be future-ready, globally aware, and confident, thanks to learning with real-world case scenarios not available elsewhere.

### Target Customer and Positioning

**Age Range:** Children ages 8–18 across 100+ countries

**Geographic Focus:** Global reach, with many students from India (where the company was founded) and other English-speaking regions such as the US, UK, and Asia-Pacific.

**Ideal Learner Persona:** A curious, bright child who is not fully challenged by standard school curriculum and whose parents seek 21st-century skills enrichment.

**Typical Parent Persona:** Globally-minded, educated, and eager to invest in their child's holistic development beyond academics.

**Positioning:** Positioned as a premium, world-class offering – branded as "the world's best live classes for children" – yet accessible online anywhere. Early Steps pitches itself as a broad supplement to formal education, covering business, finance, science, technology, and life skills.

It occupies a niche in focusing on "new-age subjects" and soft skills rarely taught in schools, while positioning itself as after-school and complementary to formal schooling. The branding (founded by a Harvard alumna, with mentors from Harvard/Stanford) further positions it as an aspirational, elite program accessible to ambitious middle-class families.

### Product and Pedagogy

**Format:** Live, online enrichment curriculum delivered through small-group classes
- Classes conducted live via video (e.g., Zoom), with 3–6 students per class
- 1 hour per week per course
- 7-level course sequence; each level runs 4–8 weeks, spanning about one year total
- Students typically meet once weekly

**Teaching Methodologies:**

Early Steps uses a proprietary case study method adapted for young learners. Every concept is taught via real-world cases and open-ended problems (similar to Harvard Business School classes, but age-appropriate).

- Classes are discussion-based and participatory, with instructors guiding students to analyze scenarios, brainstorm solutions, and communicate their ideas
- Emphasis on higher-order thinking – students learn to debate, question, and draw connections rather than memorizing content
- Strong focus on project-based learning: each level includes cases or projects that students work through and present
- Instructors (called facilitators or mentors) come from strong academic/professional backgrounds
- Low student-teacher ratio for personalized attention

**Technology:** Early Steps Academy has developed an "algorithmic classroom learning framework" to enhance the live class experience. The platform likely means a custom learning engine that adapts case content and discussion prompts to student level. Outside of live sessions, students have access to an online portal for assignments or reading materials, though most learning occurs synchronously. The program does not rely on AI tutors or gamified apps; instead, it differentiates itself through R&D-driven curriculum design (a full-time team that creates and refines case studies). Technology enables the global classroom aspect, with students from different countries joining the same class, bringing diverse perspectives. The platform also tracks outcomes.

**Overall Pedagogy:** A blend of inquiry-based learning, Socratic discussion, and real-world simulation, delivered through a virtual classroom setting.

### Curriculum Design

Early Steps Academy offers a comprehensive 7-level curriculum spanning modern knowledge domains and key skills.

**Content:**

On the content side, it covers "New-Age Subjects" including:
- Entrepreneurship
- AI & Cryptocurrency
- Space Technology
- Climate Science
- Personal Finance
- Applied Math
- Digital World
- And more

Each level of the program introduces a cluster of these subjects around a theme. Examples:
- **Level 1:** "Solve and Speak Challenge" – exposure to current affairs and global ventures
- **Level 2:** "Start Your Own Venture" – entrepreneurship basics
- **Level 3:** "Success in the 21st Century" – survey of future technologies (space, crypto, genes, etc.)
- **Level 5:** "Money Matters" – personal finance

Students must progress through all levels in order; the program is likened to school grade levels where skipping is not allowed because each part is considered essential knowledge.

**Skills:** The curriculum explicitly builds "4C Skills":
- Critical Thinking
- Communication (in English)
- Collaborative Attitude
- Creativity

Every case study and class discussion is crafted to exercise these soft skills. For example, a case about an environmental problem will require critical analysis, group discussion (collaboration), creative problem-solving, and presenting a solution (communication).

**Outcomes:** According to parent surveys, 97% reported their child's school grades improved after joining Early Steps. The curriculum is geared towards making students "college and career ready." By encountering concepts like case interviews and discussing how Google or McKinsey operate, even a middle-schooler starts understanding what high-performing careers demand.

**Age Differentiation:**

Early Steps differentiates by age group with two tracks:
- **"Little Geniuses"** (ages 8–12)
- **"Geniuses"** (13–18)

Both age groups cover the same 7 levels and subjects, but the depth and complexity are tailored to age. Case studies, classroom lessons, and assignment depth differ, allowing an 11-year-old and a 16-year-old to both learn about entrepreneurship or climate change but with different scopes and expectations.

### Business Model

**Revenue Model:**

Early Steps operates on a direct-to-consumer subscription model for its courses. Parents enroll their child and typically pay fees on a monthly or package basis.

- The company offers a free trial class as a hook – allowing the child to attend one live session at no cost
- After the trial, the core offering is sold as a multi-month program (7 levels) which can be paid in installments
- Starting price: ~₹2,000 per month (approximately USD $25/month) in the Indian market
- Likely tiered pricing by geography for other regions

**Pricing Plans:**

Early Steps packages its offering into different plans – "Expert, Master, Legend" – corresponding to a certain number of topics and duration.

- Example: An Expert plan might be a shorter module (3 months) covering 10 topics
- Legend plan is a full-year subscription covering the entire curriculum (40+ topics in 52 weeks)

This tiered approach allows parents to choose a smaller commitment or the full program, and suggests a subscription with curriculum milestones rather than one-time course purchases.

**Payment Structure:**

Service is offered on a subscription basis (monthly/quarterly payments) with some services available for free trial or promotional discounts.

There is likely an incentive to pay for longer durations upfront (e.g., discounted bundles of multiple levels). While exact pricing for each plan is not publicly listed, Early Steps emphasizes value ("1000+" topics and numerous skills for the price). Given the promise of long-term engagement, many parents opt to continue through all levels, implying high customer lifetime value.

**Sales and Marketing:**

Early Steps uses a mix of digital marketing and high-touch sales:
- Initial customer acquisition through online channels – social media ads, content marketing (blogs, YouTube webinars), and parent word-of-mouth
- "Try a Free Class" CTA on the website as a key lead generator
- Consultative sales approach: company counselors follow up with interested parents to enroll the child
- Focus on outcomes and satisfaction – cites statistics like "94% of parents say their child is more confident"
- High retention (92% retention rate) suggests subscription model where students stay enrolled across multiple months/levels, providing recurring revenue

**Partnerships and Channels:**

The business model is primarily direct (own platform). Early Steps is backed by venture investors and was part of Sequoia Capital's Spark program, which provides mentorship and grant funding – indirectly boosting its brand to customers.

The sales model is largely self-serve online (parents sign up on the website) augmented by a sales team that engages leads from the free trials. The company also leverages referrals, as evidenced by organic growth and high retention.

**Funding:**

Early Steps raised a $1.2M seed round in Nov 2021 from investors like BEENEXT (Singapore), Whiteboard Capital (India), and others. This infusion has been used to scale operations and marketing.

**Summary:**

Early Steps Academy's business model is B2C SaaS-like (education as a service) with monthly subscriptions, a freemium entry point, and a focus on delivering ongoing value to keep families subscribed through the entire multi-level curriculum. The typical price point (₹2,000 or ~$25 per month in India) places it in a mid-premium segment – affordable to urban middle-class families. With venture funding in place, Early Steps is likely prioritizing growth and scale over short-term profitability, aiming to acquire a large base of students globally.

**Early Traction:**

**Platform and Marketplace Presence:**

Early Steps distributes its classes via its own platform/website, not through third-party marketplaces. Unlike some competitors, it is not listed on platforms like Outschool or Coursera for kids. All classes are conducted through Early Steps' proprietary system or Zoom integration, and all content is internally developed.

**Scale and Operational Growth:**

Indicators of scale include:
- Learners on 4 continents (USA, Africa, UK, India)

**Growth Challenges and Scaling:**

To handle growth, Early Steps likely expanded its instructor pool and support staff. Classes being live means adding students requires adding more class sessions and teachers. Given the curriculum is uniform and centralized, scaling is about replicating the class experience across time zones. Early Steps might have teachers in different countries to cover various time zones and languages, though classes are in English. The centralized R&D ensures quality control as they grow.

The tone switches between speaking to parents' logic (e.g., citing "70% higher retention of core concepts") and their emotion (e.g., "make your child part of a global community of learners").

6. **Confidence-Building Focus:** Key marketing language revolves around confidence-building – words like "confidence," "leadership," "higher-order skills," "think and communicate confidently" appear repeatedly. The brand promises transformation of the child's persona and capabilities, positioning Moonpreneur as a place where every child becomes more confident and future-ready.

## Moonpreneur

### Value Proposition

Moonpreneur's value proposition is centered on preparing children for the future of work by blending technology, entrepreneurship, and life skills. The core problem it addresses is that traditional schools are not equipping kids with the skills needed for an AI-driven, rapidly changing world.

Moonpreneur articulates this clearly: **"By 2035, 80% of jobs will transform. Schools aren't equipped to train kids with the skills to thrive. Moonpreneur bridges the gap with future-ready learning."**

**Promised Outcomes:**

1. **Technical Innovation Skills:** Kids gain hands-on experience in STEM fields like robotics, coding, game development, and app development. They "learn, build and present real tech prototypes (Robotics, Apps, Games, AI/ML etc.)" under expert guidance.

2. **Soft "Power" Skills:** Moonpreneur emphasizes leadership, communication, resilience, and creativity. In the age of AI, technical skills may have a short shelf life but human skills drive success. The outcome promised is a confident child who can lead, speak, and collaborate – not just a coding whiz.

3. **Entrepreneurial & Career Skills:** Moonpreneur ignites the "entrepreneurial spirit" in kids. Through its curriculum, children experience "Shark Tank-style" pitching to industry experts and work on business modeling and strategy. A concrete outcome is that "40+ youth-led startups incubated" through its initiatives. Additionally, Moonpreneur has a College Counsellor component – explicitly tying its program to building a standout portfolio for college admissions. The promise is that starting early with Moonpreneur will give a student a competitive edge for university entry.

4. **Emotional & Personal Growth:** Moonpreneur aims to future-proof kids not just with skills but with the right mindset for a changing world. It plays on emotional triggers by educating parents about the gig economy and AI disruption.

**Emotional Triggers:**

- **Sense of urgency and fear:** If most kids are "still learning yesterday's rules" in school, your child will be left behind in the AI era
- **Aspirational trigger:** Parents are told US colleges want consistent growth over years, not last-minute cramming; by starting in 6th grade you're being a strategic parent
- **Entrepreneurial appeal:** The idea of their child innovating, pitching to tech gurus, maybe even founding a startup at 15 is exciting and novel

**Summary:** Moonpreneur's value proposition is holistic future readiness – combining the best of STEM learning (hard skills) with "power skills" (soft skills) so that a child is well-rounded, innovative, and prepared for whatever the future job landscape throws at them.

### Target Customer and Positioning

**Age Range:** Children aged 7–18 (core demographic for programs)

**Geographic Focus:**

- Strong presence in the United States and other English-speaking countries
- Headquarters in Silicon Valley (San Jose, CA)
- Contact numbers for US, Australia, UK, and Canada
- In-person workshops in 750+ cities worldwide
- Actively seeking partners in the UK, Singapore, Australia, and UAE
- Special programs for older students in India and South America (through ELDP initiative)

**Market Segment:** Positions itself as offering "world-class education at affordable prices," with learning starting at ~$99/month. This pricing is moderate in Western markets (cheaper than many private robotics classes or 1:1 tutoring) but expensive for emerging markets, suggesting Moonpreneur mainly targets middle to upper-middle class families in developed economies and affluent segments elsewhere.

**Ideal Learner Persona:** A child who is curious about technology or business, enjoys creative projects, and is eager to go beyond school basics. Perhaps a 10-year-old who loves Lego robotics or a 15-year-old who tinkers with coding. The program is also pitched at kids who might not yet be techies by highlighting leadership and communication.

**Ideal Parent Persona:** Likely a professional in STEM or business fields who understands the importance of innovation (e.g., engineers, entrepreneurs, doctors, corporate managers). These parents are forward-thinking, somewhat dissatisfied with traditional rote education, and actively seek opportunities to give their child an edge. They resonate with concepts like AI, gig economy, and entrepreneurship.

**Positioning:** Moonpreneur is positioned as both broad and niche. It is broad because it covers a wide range of subjects – from advanced math to robotics to soft skills – under one umbrella (unlike, say, a pure coding bootcamp or a pure public speaking course). Yet, it's niche because the integration of these elements (STEM + entrepreneurship + college prep) is not commonly found. Moonpreneur aligns itself with outcomes like building a college portfolio and positions itself almost as a necessity for college-bound students who want to stand out.

**Summary:** Moonpreneur's target customers are globally dispersed but united by a mindset: parents and children who value innovation, practical skills, and early career development. It is positioned as a globally-leading program with Silicon Valley pedigree that is accessible and affordable enough for wide adoption.

### Product and Pedagogy

**Format:**

Moonpreneur offers a hybrid learning platform combining live instruction with technology-driven personalization. The product consists of various programs (Robotics, App Development, Game Development, Advanced Math, STEM Debate, Leadership workshops, etc.) which can be taken individually or in combination.

- Each program typically includes live online classes (instructor-led sessions) and supplemental activities or content on the platform
- For example, the Robotics course includes 1.5 hours/week of live tech sessions plus 1.5 hours/month of "Power Skills" sessions (soft skills)
- Small-group classes (likely 4–6 students per instructor)
- Live sessions are approximately 90 minutes for technical courses

**Project-Based Learning:**

Moonpreneur's pedagogy is distinctive in that it integrates multiple learning methodologies:

1. **Project-Based Learning:** Every student works on projects (tech prototypes, business ideas, math problem-solving projects). This culminates in events like the MoonBattle Conference where students pitch their projects or compete, replicating real-life competitive scenarios.

2. **Inquiry and Debate:** The STEM Debate program indicates a focus on critical thinking – students research topics and engage in structured debates, honing their argumentation and reasoning.

3. **Mentorship and Experiential Learning:** Kids get to present to or interact with "tech gurus, industry stalwarts, and entrepreneurs" in a Shark Tank style setting. This exposure provides mentorship-like feedback and a taste of real-world expectations.

4. **Adaptive Learning with AI:** Moonpreneur touts an "AI-powered hybrid platform" that creates personalized learning paths. Outside of live classes, the platform likely uses quizzes, exercises, or AI-driven tools to reinforce learning at the student's pace (e.g., in Math Mastery, the platform might adapt to a child's strengths/weaknesses with adaptive difficulty; in coding, perhaps AI tools help with code suggestions or debugging).

**Use of Technology:**

- **LMS and Gamification:** They have a system called "MoonCampaign" which includes "gamification & achievement system" and "skills assessment & growth tracking"
- **AI Tutors/Tools:** "AI-driven personalized learning" and "AI-powered tools" in guiding projects
- **Collaboration Tech:** Virtual collaboration spaces (forums, group breakout sessions, etc.)
- **Physical Kits and Resources:** In robotics and product design, students use physical components. Moonpreneur facilitates this through its MoonStore

**Pedagogy Summary:**

Moonpreneur's pedagogy is experiential, project-centric, and blended (online + offline, human + AI). Students learn theory briefly then immediately apply it in creating something or solving a problem. They emphasize consistent practice over years (not a one-off camp but a continuum), which is pedagogically aimed at mastery and internalization of skills. The use of events (competitions, showcases, conferences) creates motivational milestones for students.

### Curriculum Design

**Three Broad Domains:**

Moonpreneur's curriculum is structured as a multi-year, modular journey covering three broad domains:

1. **STEM (Tech Innovations):**
   - Robotics, Advanced Robotics (IoT), Coding/App Development, Game Development
   - Available to ages 7–18, tiered by difficulty (Tier-0 for beginners through advanced tiers)
   - Coverage from fundamental concepts (circuits, motors) to advanced topics (prototyping, testing, future tech applications)
   - Philosophy: students don't just learn theory, but must create something tangible (e.g., build a robot, publish a simple app, design a game level)

2. **Math Mastery:**
   - Advanced Math & Data Analysis, Vedic & Mental Math, Applied Mathematics in Tech
   - Grade-based (roughly 2nd–8th grade levels)
   - Focus on speed and understanding: mental arithmetic techniques, logical problem solving, competitive exam prep (like Kangaroo or AMC contests)
   - Spiral approach reinforcing school concepts but teaching faster methods and advanced problems
   - Includes game-based and contest preparation

3. **Power Skills & Leadership (Soft Skills):**
   - Entrepreneurship, leadership, communication, financial literacy
   - **STEM Debate Program:** Research, forming evidence-backed opinions, public speaking, panel discussions, Mini Debate League competition
   - **Financial Literacy** (ages 9–14): 12-month course on money management, personal finance, investing
   - **Entrepreneurship/Business:** Students learn business modeling, pitching, marketing, and launch real teen-led ventures (2000+ pitches by young innovators, 40+ youth-led startups incubated)
   - **ELDP** (Entrepreneurship and Leadership Development Program): For ages 18–23 in partnership with universities

**Curriculum Progression:**

- Tier-based (e.g., Robotics has Tier 0 through Tier 3 or 4)
- Students advance tier by tier each year
- Math is grade-based at a more advanced plane
- Soft skills programs (debate, financial literacy) are one-off series with specific durations (6 or 12 months)
- Overarching "Innovator Program" connects all streams as a "melting pot of technology, STEM & soft skills"

**Integration with Real-World Outcomes:**

- Each course or module typically ends with a capstone event or certification
- After completing programs, students have a portfolio (e.g., collection of projects, robots built, apps developed, leadership activities) meant to strengthen college applications
- Partnerships with universities (15+ including OP Jindal, Plaksha, FLAME) give extra weightage to applicants who completed Moonpreneur projects

### Business Model

**Revenue Model & Pricing:**

Moonpreneur sells its programs through monthly or yearly subscription plans with modular pricing:

- **Single program** (e.g., Robotics): ~$149/month (includes weekly 1.5-hour live tech classes plus monthly soft skills session)
- **Math:** ~$169/month
- **Combo packages** (Robotics + Math): ~$229/month (discounted to encourage multi-subject enrollment)
- Entry-level offerings when billed annually: $99/month effective
- One-time registration fee: ~$29
- First month free promotional offer
- **Annual commitments:** "Unlock 25% savings with yearly plan"

Average customer LTV is reportedly 22 months (nearly 2 years of subscription on average).

**Product Sales:**

Moonpreneur has additional product sales via MoonStore including physical kits (robotics kits, DIY project kits) and a branded board game teaching entrepreneurial skills.

**Free Workshops & Lead Generation:**

Moonpreneur runs free in-person workshops in many cities (e.g., free robotics and math workshops). These act as live demos of the program and serve as marketing activities. Rather than spend solely on ads, Moonpreneur invests in on-ground events to acquire customers. These partnerships help them scale – boasting 500+ workshops monthly and presence in 750+ cities globally.

**Sales Model:**

- Initial sales funnel often starts online (parents might sign up for a free trial class or local workshop on the website)
- Transitions to consultative sale with inside sales team or education counselors
- Direct purchase through the site with credit card is also supported

**Partnerships (Distribution Model):**

A standout aspect is the **Marketing Partner Program**, akin to a franchise or affiliate model. Individuals or businesses become local partners who host free workshops in their area and help recruit students, in exchange for a share of revenue.

- Partners pay a startup fee ($5k–$15k depending on location)
- Moonpreneur provides training, marketing assistance, and curriculum
- Partner hosts 2-4 workshop days a month (often on weekends)
- Moonpreneur's central team delivers the actual online program once students enroll
- Partners act as sales agents and community builders

**Retention & Upsell:**

- Monthly churn under 2.5%
- Aim for >3 year LTV
- Progression model: once a child finishes one level or course, there's always a next level or new domain to try
- Cross-selling built in (bundle pricing for taking multiple streams)

**Funding & Financials:**

- Over $4M in seed funding
- Aiming for Series A
- Projects aggressive revenue growth
- LTV/CAC ratio ~5 currently, projected 7–10 in the future
- Unit economics positive

**Summary:** Moonpreneur's business model is diversified but centered on subscriptions. The company invests heavily in growth via free value-add services (workshops) and leverages partnerships to reduce customer acquisition costs. The model is scalable (mostly online delivery) with some controlled physical presence for marketing. Multiple revenue streams (courses, kits, competitions/events) and clear upsell paths across subjects and into advanced offerings ensure growth and retention.
