# Pricing Benchmarks: Kids/Teens Soft Skills EdTech

---

## PlanetSpark

### Pricing Model

Long-term packages of live 1:1 classes in communication (public speaking, English). Typically sold as multi-month programs rather than single classes. Shorter packages (e.g. 3, 6, or 12 months) are also offered, often with "scholarship" discounts.

### Price Range

Premium-priced. Examples:
- 12-month "special package": ~₹68,000 (~$820)
- 3-month public speaking course: ₹65,000 (~$780)
- Per-class effective rates: ₹275–375 per 1:1 class
- 2-year program: $1,145 after ~49% scholarship discount

### Format & Features

- Live 1:1 tutoring with a dedicated communication coach
- Personalized curriculum, performance tracking, and extra practice sessions
- High-touch coaching model (explicitly positioned away from "low-cost, high-volume" classes)
- Free demo classes offered to entice enrollment

### Geography & Sales Model

- Founded in India, serves Indian and global markets
- B2C (parent-focused)
- In India's price-sensitive market, justifies cost with outcomes (confident communication) and teacher quality
- Discounts often framed as scholarships; emphasizes value over hourly cost

### Trials & Scholarships

- Free one-on-one trial ("demo") class is standard
- Needs-based scholarships not publicly advertised
- Substantial promotional discounts ("scholarships") used during sales
- Price sensitivity evident: parents often negotiate or expect discounts

## Outschool

### Pricing Model

Marketplace pay-per-class. Independent instructors list courses (one-time classes, multi-week courses, ongoing clubs, or private tutoring). Recently introduced membership plans with monthly fees for credits.

### Price Range

Highly variable, generally moderate to high (Western pricing):
- Live small-group classes: $5 to $40 per class
- One-on-one tutoring: $14 up to ~$60 per hour
- Most one-hour group classes: $15–$20 per learner (average ~$18/hour)
- Membership plans: ~$40–$70/month for credits covering 1–2 classes/week

### Format & Features

- Live interactive classes, primarily small-group (3–8 learners typical)
- Vast catalog covers academic subjects, soft skills, hobbies, future skills
- 1:1 tutoring available but less central
- No fixed curriculum – each teacher designs the class
- Membership program provides monthly class credits at slight discount
- No platform-wide free trials, but new users often get promo credit (e.g. $20 off)

### Geography & Sales Model

- Headquarters in U.S., serves global B2C audience (classes in English)
- Pricing usually in USD
- Regional price sensitivity addressed by allowing teachers to set lower prices
- Also partners with some schools (B2B2C channel), but core model is direct-to-parent

### Trials & Special Offers

- No standard free trial classes (each class is a paid product)
- Money-back satisfaction guarantee for classes that don't meet expectations
- Limited membership trials (e.g. $0 first month as promotion)
- Scholarships: Through nonprofit arm, some learners from underserved communities receive credits/grants

## 98thPercentile

### Pricing Model

Structured course subscriptions sold in multi-week bundles. Programs (Math, English (ELA), Coding, Public Speaking) run as small-group live classes with fixed schedules. Parents typically prepay for 12-week (quarter) or 24-week (half-year) packages.

### Price Range

Mid-range, with per-class rates ~$15–$19 (USD pricing):

| Program | 12 Weeks | 24 Weeks | Per-Class Rate |
|---------|----------|----------|-----------------|
| Math (Grades K–8) | $357 (24 classes) | $678.30 (48 classes) | ~$14–$15 |
| English ELA | $447 (24 classes) | $849.30 (48 classes) | ~$18 |
| Coding | ~$357 (12-week term) | — | ~$15 |
| Public Speaking | $357 (12 classes/week) | $678.30 (24 classes) | ~$28–$30 |

Limited-time deals (e.g. "$3 first class" trial) offered to attract new students.

### Format & Features

- Live online classes in small groups
- Math/ELA/Coding cohorts capped at 4 students per class
- Public speaking runs with up to 6 students
- Classes typically 50 minutes, held 2x/week for math/ELA/coding; 1x/week for public speaking
- Packages include adaptive homework, weekly assessments, extra help sessions, progress reports
- U.S.-standard curricula (e.g. ELA taught by American teachers with Indian teaching assistants)
- Free trial classes offered to new customers

### Geography & Sales Model

- Based in Texas, USA, but markets heavily to North American and Indian diaspora
- All prices in USD; schedules cater to U.S. time zones
- Primary B2C targeting of U.S. families and international families seeking American curriculum
- Per-class pricing (~$15/hour) leverages blend of U.S. and India-based staff to control costs
- Higher-end market in India; customers tend to be in top metros or seeking U.S.-style education

### Trials & Discounts

- Free trial sessions for each subject are standard
- Periodic promotional discounts (seasonal sales, referral credits) available
- Messaging highlights "affordable" quality (e.g. math ~$89/month in some ads)

## KidsKey

### Pricing Model

Lesson packs for 1:1 tutoring. Parents purchase prepaid packages (4, 8, 16, or 32 lessons) valid for up to 1 year with flexible scheduling.

### Price Range

Affordable per class with bulk discounts (USD pricing):

| Package Size | Per-Class Cost | Total Cost (est.) |
|--------------|----------------|-------------------|
| 4 lessons | $20.00 | $80 |
| 8 lessons | $16.90 | $135 |
| 16 lessons | $14.90 | $238 |
| 32 lessons | $13.90 | ~$445 |

Lessons are 25–40 minutes depending on course and age.

### Format & Features

- One-on-one live classes (no prerecorded videos)
- Covers subjects from phonics and reading (Russian/English) to math
- Each lesson includes live instruction plus assigned interactive homework or printables
- Flexible scheduling/cancellation with 8 hours notice (no credit loss)
- Unused lessons can be carried over for up to 1 year
- Free trial lesson offered to all new students

### Geography & Sales Model

- Focus on Russian-speaking and bilingual children
- Legally based in Cyprus with U.S. contact, indicating globally distributed client base
- Serves Russian diaspora in North America, Europe, and domestic Eastern European learners
- B2C international model with pricing in USD
- Personalized tutoring allows charging in USD (moderately priced for Western customers, accessible to upper-middle-class in primary regions)

### Trials & Policies

- First lesson free for any course
- No formal scholarships advertised
- Generous refund policy: unused lessons refundable within 180 days
- Declining per-lesson cost for larger packages addresses cost sensitivity

## House of Soft Skills (HOSS)

### Pricing Model

Pay-per-class (with ongoing enrollment). Live courses in soft skills and communication, priced per class in USD. Example: Soft Skills Development (ages 4–14): $13/class; English + Personality Development (ages 7–14): $10/class.

Monthly cost estimate: ~$40–$52/month (assuming 1 class per week).

### Price Range

Moderate (USD pricing):
- Soft Skills Development: $13 per class
- English + Personality Development: $10 per class

For Indian customers, $10+ per class (~₹800+) is relatively premium for group lessons, but HOSS emphasizes very small group sizes and specialized content to justify pricing.

### Format & Features

- Live small-group classes deliberately kept very small (up to 3 students per class)
- Courses target skills like communication, teamwork, etiquette, and confidence building
- Instructor-led with interactive activities
- Often integrates soft skills with language learning (phonics or spoken English + personality development)
- Highlights "social learning" benefits in 3-student classes
- Free trial classes can be booked before enrollment

### Geography & Sales Model

- Based in India (contact: +91 phone, .co.in email)
- Targets both Indian and international families
- All pricing given in USD, signaling premium positioning in India
- B2C (direct to parents) extracurricular enrichment provider
- Regional pricing strategy: USD pricing indicates target of less price-sensitive customers (metro Indian parents, NRIs)
- Small classes + accreditation (e.g. ISO certified curriculum) build value narrative around quality

### Trials & Offers

- Free trial class for any interested student
- Clear pricing ("fee starting from $13 per class")
- No formal scholarship or financial aid programs mentioned
- Value proposition (21st-century skills = future success) used to justify cost

## Clever Harvey

### Pricing Model

Fixed-price courses and bundled programs. Specializes in short "Junior MBA" programs (2–4 weeks) and larger bundles (FutureCEO Club, College Catapult). Single courses or discounted multi-course packs available.

### Price Range

Mid to high (for Indian market):

| Program | Duration | Price |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Single JuniorMBA | 15 live sessions / 3 weeks | ₹10,000 (~$120) |
| Chief ___ Officer bundles | — | ₹30,000 each |
| JuniorCMO bundle | — | ₹45,000 |
| FutureCEO Club | ~1–2 years | ₹75,000 (~$900) |
| College Catapult | — | ₹500,000 (~$6,000) |

Core teen skill courses: ~₹10k each. Combination programs: ₹30k–₹75k.

### Format & Features

- Cohort-based live bootcamps for teens (Grades 8–12)
- Each JuniorMBA: 15 hours total (1-hour classes over 3 weeks), usually taught to 6–15 students
- Courses created with industry partners (e.g. Advertising with Domino's) for real-world project feel
- Deliverables (business project, app prototype) and certificate provided
- Flexible scheduling: new batches start every Monday; families choose convenient timing (purchases valid 6–12 months)
- Money-back guarantee: full refund if unsatisfied within first 3 sessions

### Geography & Sales Model

- Based in India, targeting Indian high-schoolers (Grades 8–12)
- Content appeals to students aiming for competitive colleges or global careers
- B2C online marketing to parents and teens
- Also B2B2C through schools (College Catapult counseling service)
- Regional pricing: ₹10,000 is premium for India; emphasis on unique value (Ivy League alumni instructors, real company case studies, certificates recognized by some universities)
- Installment options available; free webinars/workshops generate interest
- Relatively high renewal rate indicates strong perceived value among affluent segment

### Trials & Scholarships

- No standard free trial classes
- Guaranteed refunds and short commitment units (3-week minimum) mitigate risk
- Free introductory sessions or contests (innovation challenges) serve as lead generators
- Premium "career discovery" positioning justifies high fees

## Early Steps Academy

### Pricing Model

Subscription-based cohorts with multi-tier curriculum. Three levels (Expert, Master, Legend) with tiered pricing and options for installment payment. Effectively monthly subscription with 5, 9, or 12-month commitment options.

### Price Range

Budget-friendly (high volume model):

| Tier | Duration | Total Cost | Monthly Equivalent |
|------|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Expert | ~5 months (10 topics) | ₹14,250 | ₹2.5k–₹3k/month |
| Master | 9 months (20 topics) | ₹23,000 | ₹2.5k–₹3k/month |
| Legend | 12 months (~40 topics) | ₹30,000 | ₹2.5k–₹3k/month |

Translates to ~₹600–₹750 (~$7–$9) per class (assuming 1 class/week). International pricing reportedly $18–$25/week, reflecting higher pricing for non-Indian markets.

### Format & Features

- Small-group live classes (3–6 students) held 1x per week (1 hour)
- Curriculum: 1000+ "new-age" subjects (crypto, space tech, entrepreneurship, climate, etc.) taught via Harvard case study method adapted for kids
- Structured in 7 sequential levels covering different themes and skills (each level ~8 weeks)
- Age-based grouping: 8–12 ("Little Geniuses") and 13–18 ("Geniuses") with age-tailored content
- Includes weekly assignments, international "Global Championships" (competitions), certificates per level, final Letter of Recommendation
- Free demo classes heavily advertised to encourage sign-ups

### Geography & Sales Model

- Based in Bangalore, India; positions itself as global program (students from 7+ countries as of 2022)
- B2C targeting progressive parents worldwide
- In India, competes on value: low monthly cost (comparable to typical mid-range after-school activity) with high retention (92%)
- Volume-focused strategy; tiered packages allow gradual commitment (3-week "Expert" module at ~₹2k/week, then upgrade to longer packages)
- Messaging: "the world's best case-study classes for kids" helps justify higher cost vs. regular tuition
- Likely adjusts pricing by region (U.S./Middle East may see higher rates)

### Trials & Value Perception

- Free trial class offered to every interested family (crucial for unconventional program)
- No explicit scholarships; uniform pricing signals fairness and quality
- Regional pricing differences: $18–$25/week in Forbes (2022) suggests higher international pricing
- Domestic India pricing ₹2k–₹3k/month considered accessible for urban middle-class
- Value framing: early exposure to future-ready topics = better college admissions and careers

## Moonpreneur

### Pricing Model

Monthly subscription plans (recurring) for STEM and leadership courses, with annual prepay discount option. Also fixed-length programs (e.g. holiday bootcamps) with one-time fees.

### Price Range

Mid-range (U.S.-aligned pricing):

| Program | Monthly Cost | Annual Discount |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Single course (Robotics) | $149 | — |
| Math | $169 | — |
| Robotics + Math combo | $229 | — |
| Introductory offers | "Starting at $99/month" (in some promotions) | — |
| First month trial | Free (with $29 registration) | — |
| Summer MoonCamp (3-month) | — | $499 total |

Roughly $20–$30 per instructional hour. Often advertises "First month free" with nominal registration fee.

### Format & Features

- Live online classes in small groups (typically 4–6 students)
- Standard subscription: 1.5 hours/week live tech/STEM instruction + 1.5 hours/month "Power Skills" (soft-skill/leadership workshops)
- Math plans include additional 1.5 hours/week optional competitive math prep for older kids
- Differentiates through STEM + soft skills in same subscription
- Some programs have fixed durations (e.g. 6-month debate club for $99/month after $29 first month)
- Free trials heavily promoted ("Book a Free Trial")
- Sales funnel: free trial → discounted first month → regular billing
- Additional value: contests (MoonBattle) and student showcase platform included in subscription

### Geography & Sales Model

- Silicon Valley (USA) company operating globally
- Pricing and marketing skew toward North America (USD, references to U.S. cities)
- Strictly B2C online (no school licensing); partners with local community centers for events
- Messaging: "world-class education at affordable prices" (~$150/month for live STEM positioned as "affordable" relative to U.S. markets)
- Regional strategy: More scholarships/steep first-month discounts in India and price-sensitive regions (e.g. $9 first month, then $139 for subsequent months)
- Two-part pricing reflects price-trial sensitivity; once parents see progress, may accept higher ongoing cost

### Trials & Scholarships

- Free trial classes standard for all courses
- Scholarship contests (innovation competitions where winners get course credits)
- Referral program (friends get discounted joiners' fee)
- "First month free" with small signup fee = promotional scholarship to lower initial barrier
- Regional price localization: U.S. market tolerates $149/month; cost-sensitive regions offered lower-tier plans ($99/month start)
- Perceived value in comprehensive curriculum (technical + soft skills) and tangible outputs (kids build robots, games, entrepreneurial projects)

## Regional Price Sensitivity & Value Perception

### Market Dynamics

**Geography-based pricing:**
- **U.S.-focused companies** (Outschool, Moonpreneur) charge higher absolute prices per hour due to higher disposable incomes and culture of investing in extracurriculars
- **India-based programs** (Early Steps, Clever Harvey) keep sticker prices lower (often under $40/month or a few hundred dollars per course) to attract volume
- **Bridge strategies:** Some India-origin companies use differential pricing (PlanetSpark, 98thPercentile, HOSS quote in USD and highlight quality to justify premium-for-India but moderate-by-Western prices)

### Perceived Value Factors

**Format and outcomes impact pricing:**
- **1:1 personalized coaching** (e.g. PlanetSpark) commands top dollar due to intensive attention and faster skill gains (parents in India report ₹5,000–₹7,000/month for weekly 1:1 classes)
- **Small-group interactive** (Outschool, Early Steps) positioned as enriching but less bespoke; priced per class/month comparable to hiring a tutor or joining a club
- **Self-paced or large-group** formats tend to be cheapest

**Promise of tangible outcomes** increases willingness to pay:
- Programs promising certificates, portfolio projects, competition wins (Clever Harvey, Early Steps) help parents view higher fees as "investment" in child's profile
- Framing as stepping stones to college/careers (rather than just classes) justifies premium pricing

### Trial Strategy Differences

**Price-sensitive regions:** Free classes or heavy first-month discounts crucial for customer acquisition; lower risk for skeptical parents

**Higher-income markets:** Free trials more about demonstrating quality in crowded market

**Examples:**
- Outschool doesn't need to give classes away (parents accustomed to paying), but new platforms like Moonpreneur entering U.S. use free month to build trust
- PlanetSpark offers free demos globally, but uses "50% scholarship" rhetoric especially in India to make large packages feel like a deal

### Localized Pricing Examples

- **Outschool $15 group class:** Great value in U.S. for unique topic
- **India $12-per-class equivalent (₹1,000):** Parent may balk; Early Steps' ₹500/class works in India's context by emphasizing global, R&D-backed content
- **House of Soft Skills $13/class:** Targets elite clientele prioritizing soft skills

### Package Design for Price Sensitivity

Companies craft offerings to align with customer base price sensitivity:

**Higher price sensitivity:** Smaller, more flexible offerings
- KidsKey's 4-lesson pack
- Early Steps' monthly cost breakdown

**Lower price sensitivity:** Longer subscriptions and memberships
- Outschool's monthly membership
- Moonpreneur's quarterly billing options

## Summary: Pricing Spectrum

**Ultra-premium 1:1 coaching:**
- PlanetSpark: ~$10–$15/hour in India (quite high by local standards)

**Mid-range group learning:**
- 98thPercentile, Outschool, Moonpreneur: $15–$30/hour

**Budget-friendly high-volume:**
- Early Steps: ~$2/hour in large annual bundles

**Key insight:** Each company finds a pricing sweet spot by considering format (1:1 vs group), promised outcome (academic support vs future-ready skills), and target geography's ability to pay. Providers highlight different "value" types—personal growth, certificates, global exposure, convenience, or fun—to justify price points and adjust when expanding to new regions.

The result is a diverse range of coexisting pricing models, each catering to a segment of the highly heterogeneous market for kids' soft and future skills education.
