# Pimpi Card — Brand Brief v1.1

**Do chores. Earn stamps. Build your card. Spend on things that actually matter to you.**

---

## The One-Line Pitch

Pimpi Card is the reward economy of the Pimpi universe — a household stamp wallet where kids earn by doing and spend on what they love, powered by parents behind the scenes.

---

## The Core Loop

Pimpi Card runs on two layers that work together: **Chores** give kids something to do right now; **Stamp Cards** give them something to build over time.

### Layer 1 — Chores (Earn Now)

The immediate earning loop. Parents assign chores, each with a stamp value. Kids complete them, stamps land in their wallet.

1. **Do a chore** — Parent-assigned task with a stamp value
2. **Earn stamps** — Stamps land in your Pimpi Card wallet
3. **Save up** — Pin a goal reward, watch progress fill
4. **Spend your card** — Request a reward, parent approves and fulfils

This is the everyday rhythm. Quick, concrete, satisfying.

### Layer 2 — Stamp Cards (Build Over Time)

The habit layer. Stamp Cards are achievement trackers tied to categories of chores. They don't replace individual chores — they sit on top of them, turning repetition into visible progress.

Each Stamp Card has a theme (e.g. "Tidy Tiger", "Kitchen Hero", "Green Thumb") and a series of milestones. Every time a kid completes a qualifying chore, the card tracks it. Hit a milestone, earn bonus stamps, and watch the card fill in.

**How a Stamp Card works:**

- A card has a title, a short description, and a stamp grid (e.g. 5 milestone slots)
- Milestones escalate: 1 → 5 → 15 → 30 → 50
- Each milestone stamps in with a date and a bonus stamp payout
- Earlier milestones are easy wins; later ones take sustained effort
- The card is never "due" — there's no deadline, no streak pressure
- A filled card is a permanent keepsake in the kid's collection

**Example: "Tidy Tiger"**

> *Your room, your rules. Keep it tidy and watch this card fill up.*

| Milestone | Trigger | Bonus |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| 🐯 ×1 | Made bed 1 time | +2 stamps |
| 🐯 ×5 | Made bed 5 times | +5 stamps |
| 🐯 ×15 | Made bed 15 times | +10 stamps |
| 🐯 ×30 | Made bed 30 times | +20 stamps |
| 🐯 ×50 | Made bed 50 times | +50 stamps |

**Why this matters:**

- Chores alone are transactional — do thing, get paid. That's fine, but it doesn't build habits.
- Stamp Cards add a long arc. Kids see cumulative effort visualised. They're not just earning stamps — they're filling in something.
- No streaks, no guilt, no punishment for skipping a day. Just progress that accrues whenever they show up.
- The mechanic is borrowed from the Nook Miles achievement system: visible milestones, escalating payouts, permanent record of what you've done.

## Spending

Stamps earned from both chores and Stamp Card milestones go into one wallet. Spending works the same regardless of how the stamps were earned:

1. **Browse rewards** — Parent-created catalog of real, personal, meaningful things
2. **Pin a goal** — Choose a reward to save toward; progress bar fills as stamps accumulate
3. **Spend your card** — When you have enough, request the reward
4. **Parent fulfils** — Parent approves and delivers the reward in real life

## Design Language

### Inherited from Pimpi

Post-it aesthetic. Bold flat colours. Adhesive strip details. Slight rotations. Handmade warmth. Same mascot.

### Unique to Pimpi Card

- Physical card object as hero UI
- Stamp grid motif — the visual of stamps filling in is central to both layers
- Wallet / pocket metaphor
- Amber as primary accent
- Stamp Card collection — a shelf or binder of cards the kid has started or completed

## Tone of Voice

**Do:**

- Warm and matter-of-fact — not hype, not exaggeration
- Respect kid autonomy — never patronising or preachy
- Concrete and physical — stamps, cards, spending; never "points", "currency", "balance"
- Satisfying, not addictive — no pushy notifications, no streaks-at-all-costs
- Celebrate accumulation, not speed — "Look how far you've come" over "Don't break your streak"

**Never:**

- Sound like a bank, a fintech, or a chore chart
- Use abstract financial language
- Talk down to kids or over-explain
- Guilt kids for inactivity or missed days

## Protected Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Stamp** | The unit of currency. Never "point", "coin", or "credit". |
| **Pimpi Card** | The app and the wallet object kids carry. Always both words, always capitalised. |
| **Spend your card** | The redemption action. Not "redeem", not "cash out". |
| **Chore** | Parent-assigned earning task. Not "quest", not "mission" (that's Pimpi's word). |
| **Reward** | What kids save up for. Parent-created. Real, personal, meaningful. |
| **Goal** | A reward the kid has pinned to save toward. |
| **Fulfil** | When a parent delivers the reward after approving the spend request. |
| **Stamp Card** | An achievement tracker tied to a category of chores. A card with milestones that fill in over time. Not "badge", not "achievement", not "challenge". |
| **Milestone** | A threshold on a Stamp Card. When hit, a stamp fills in and bonus stamps are earned. |
| **Collection** | The set of Stamp Cards a kid has started or completed. Not "trophy case", not "achievements". |

## Age Scaling

### Ages 4–7 — "My special card"

Parent sets up everything. Child sees stamps land with big satisfying animation. Rewards are simple (sticker, hug, extra story). Parent helps them "spend their card" together. Stamp Cards are visible but simple — maybe just 3 milestones, with big cheerful art. The kid mostly experiences them as surprise bonus moments: "You've tidied up 5 times! Here's a special stamp!"

### Ages 8–12 — "I'm saving up for something"

Kids browse the reward catalog independently. They pin a goal and track progress. They initiate spend requests. The delayed gratification mechanic is front and centre. Stamp Cards become a source of pride — kids check their collection, see which cards are close to the next milestone, and choose chores strategically. The card-filling visual is the hook.

### Ages 13–17 — "I manage my own economy"

Teens care about autonomy. The app should feel like their wallet, not a parent's reward chart. Higher-value chores, higher-value rewards (outings, experiences, privileges). Less visual noise, more control. Stamp Cards feel like a track record — a quiet, satisfying history of what they've consistently done. The collection view is clean, not cutesy.
