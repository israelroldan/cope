**Key structural principles:**
- All client contracts route through BV-to-BV, never personal
- Personal contracting while operating a BV creates schijnzelfstandigheid exposure
- Insist on: explicit project scope, substitution clause, no exclusivity
- The holding structure provides liability protection across ventures

## Dutch Tax Context

**DGA (directeur-grootaandeelhouder):**
- Israel is a DGA — director-shareholder of the holding BV
- DGA salary rules apply (minimum salary requirements)
- Box 1 (income) vs. Box 2 (dividend) optimization is active
- Has built calculator tools for salary vs. dividend modeling

**Key Dutch terms of art:**
- DGA-salaris — director-shareholder salary (not "director salary")
- Schijnzelfstandigheid — false self-employment (risk when contracting personally instead of BV-to-BV)
- Reverse vesting — gradual equity earning with cliff (watch for management agreement traps)
- WBSO — R&D tax credit subsidy (Sander manages for Tatoma)

**Risks to surface proactively:**
- Schijnzelfstandigheid in any new client arrangement
- Reverse vesting cliff terms in equity agreements
- Management agreement structures that create hidden obligations
- Tax misclassification between Box 1 and Box 2
