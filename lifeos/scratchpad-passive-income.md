## Strategic Pivot: TLOM Folds Into Pimpi

**Decision (2026-03-23):** Rather than pursuing TLOM as a standalone venture
with a disengaging team, absorb the EdTech concept into the Pimpi universe
as **Pimpi Learn**. Reuse the content pipeline, drop the facilitator/cohort
dependency, make it self-directed and lighter weight.

**Why:**
- TLOM team (education consultant, sales/PM) losing interest — can't depend on them
- Pimpi already has the philosophy, brand, live app, and audience
- Self-directed beats cohort-based when you don't have facilitators
- Reduces venture count (5 → 4), one less context switch
- Content pipeline transfers cleanly (generates Grejs + Deep Dives instead of facilitator scripts)
- Three-product bundle makes subscription more compelling than any single product

### Pimpi Learn — three layers

1. **Grej of the Day** — daily micro-learning. One interesting topic, beautifully
   presented. No guilt, no streak punishment. Kid can save, share, or go deeper.
   Content pipeline generates these at scale. Start curated by topic (science,
   history, nature, culture, tech), evolve to personalized based on kid's
   interests and Library of Me profile.

2. **Deep Dives** — kid finds something interesting, chooses to explore it.
   Structured 3-5 step path, age-appropriate, produces an artifact. Uses TLOM
   content pipeline. Desire-led, not curriculum-led.

3. **Library of Me** — self-reflection layer. Soft skills, values, identity, growth.
   The observe-map-design-test-codify cycle. 7 competencies as organizing framework.
   Over time, the kid builds their personal operating manual.

### Pimpi Explore — hobby catalog + future marketplace

**Phase 1 (content only):** Curated "dopamine menu" — categorized hobbies and
activities with descriptions, difficulty levels, what you need to start, and
connections to Sparks/Card/Learn. Fights "I'm bored and don't know what to do."

**Phase 2 (marketplace):** Local hobby clubs, teachers, and stores can list
themselves. Parents discover local options. Businesses pay for visibility.
Network effects: more families → more businesses → more families.

### Revenue model

```
Free tier:
  Sparks  — 3 active sparks
  Card    — basic stamp tracking
  Learn   — 1 Grej per day (view only)
  Explore — browse catalog

Pimpi+ (EUR 4-6/mo or EUR 40-60/year per family):
  Sparks  — unlimited sparks, templates
  Card    — full stamp economy, categories, milestones
  Learn   — Grej archive + Deep Dives + Library of Me
  Explore — save favorites, personalized suggestions

Future: Pimpi Explore marketplace (B2B)
  Local businesses pay for listings/promoted placement
```

### Content pipeline as inventory engine

The TLOM content pipeline becomes the Pimpi content factory:
- Generate 365 Grejs (one year of daily content) as first batch
- Generate Deep Dive scaffolding for top 50 topics
- Generate Library of Me reflection prompts per competency
- Generate Explore catalog entries for 200+ hobbies
- All before a single kid signs up — content is inventory, not on-demand
- New batches add value but aren't required daily

## The Uncomfortable Math

If monthly fixed costs are EUR X and Tatoma salary covers it with little margin:

- **EUR 500/mo extra** = breathing room, emergency fund starts building
- **EUR 1,000/mo extra** = meaningful savings, can invest in ventures
- **EUR 2,500/mo extra** = could reduce Tatoma dependency, negotiate from strength

The first EUR 500 matters most. It changes the dynamic from "I need this
paycheck" to "I choose this work."

**Revenue math for Pimpi+:**
- 100 families × EUR 5/mo = EUR 500/mo ← breathing room
- 250 families × EUR 5/mo = EUR 1,250/mo ← meaningful
- 500 families × EUR 5/mo = EUR 2,500/mo ← game-changing

The content pipeline makes this possible because content is created once and
serves every subscriber forever. Marginal cost of a new family ≈ EUR 0.
