# For Your Eyes Only

> Patterns, risks, and honest observations distilled from 11 AI profiles and
> the way you work. Not criticism — situational awareness. Read when you need
> grounding, not when you need motivation.

---

## What the AIs all independently noticed

Every AI you work with — ChatGPT, Dia, Claude Chat, Claude Code across five
projects — arrived at overlapping observations without coordinating. When
multiple independent systems converge on the same signal, pay attention.

---

### 1. You are the single point of failure everywhere

Tatoma's platform? You built it. TLOM's pipeline? You built it. Pimpi's
monorepo? You built it. Cope? You built it. StudyStars? You.

Every soul file describes you as "solo founder-engineer." Your co-founders
bring ops, sales, partnerships, product thinking — but the moment you're sick,
burned out, or unavailable for two weeks, every venture stops producing. This
is not a risk you'll face someday — it's a risk you carry every day.

**What this means:** Your health, energy, and focus are not personal luxuries.
They are infrastructure. Treating them as optional is like skipping backups
on a production database.

---

### 2. Revenue concentration is dangerous

100% of income from Tatoma. Zero from StudyStars, Pimpi, Palu Labs, Cope.
You said it yourself: paycheck to paycheck, hoping the equity pays out.

The equity risk compounds with the single-point-of-failure risk. If your
relationship with the Tatoma co-founders changes, if a key client churns, if
the consulting market contracts — there is no fallback generating money.

**What this means:** Diversifying revenue is not a "nice to have" side project.
It's a survival strategy. Even a small secondary income stream (EUR 500-1000/mo)
changes the risk profile meaningfully.

---

### 3. You start brilliantly but monetize slowly

Look at what you've built: a 10-app multi-tenant SaaS platform, a
compliance-first EdTech pipeline, a kid-focused stamp economy, a personal AI
infrastructure with MCP specialists, a fiction development toolkit. The
quality is genuinely high.

But the pattern across your portfolio is: build → refine → build more → refine
more → … → monetize later. "Later" keeps getting pushed because there's always
another feature, another venture, another improvement.

TLOM has facilitator scripts but no paying cohort. Pimpi Sparks is live but
free. Pimpi Card is in private alpha. The Book Doctor is a toolkit without
customers. Cope is infrastructure for you, not a product for others.

**What this means:** You don't have a building problem. You have a finishing-
and-selling problem. The next EUR you earn should probably come from something
that already exists, not something you build next.

---

### 4. You work at a pace that isn't sustainable

55 sessions in a single day. 35K messages. 440K+ total messages since January
2026. Over 1M tokens per day at peak. This isn't hobbyist usage — this is
industrial output from a single human being who also does school runs.

Multiple AIs noted they should help you "prioritize ruthlessly" and "protect
energy." Your dia-personal profile literally says: "when he's overloaded, help
prioritize ruthlessly, suggest what to drop or defer, and propose a realistic
next 48-hour plan instead of an aspirational backlog."

The cope-platform profile names it most directly: "Israel's cognitive load is
too high for any single attention span."

**What this means:** The pace you're running is borrowing from future energy.
You know this — "protect energy, not just time" is your own principle. But
principles that get overridden in practice need enforcement, not just
documentation.

---

### 5. Your AI infrastructure is a superpower nobody else has

1000+ sessions. Specialist agent delegation. MCP integrations across school,
email, Slack, calendar, finance, lifelog. LifeOS on Sanity with 21 tools.
Custom hooks and skills in Claude Code. An orchestrator that spawns domain
specialists and aggregates their output.

Most CTOs talk about AI adoption. You live it at a scale that is genuinely
unusual. This operational knowledge — not the code, the *knowledge* of how to
actually work with AI as a co-engineer at production intensity — is rare and
valuable.

You're sitting on it. Nobody's paying for this expertise except Tatoma clients,
who get it bundled into consulting.

---

### 6. The "little equity" at Tatoma deserves scrutiny

Your claude-chat profile mentions you "independently identified legal risks in
the proposed management agreement structure" and evaluated reverse vesting
cliffs. Your principles include "make the implicit explicit" about
schijnzelfstandigheid and management agreement traps.

The fact that multiple AI profiles reference your vigilance about equity
structures suggests this has been on your mind repeatedly. If the equity
position is genuinely small AND the legal structure has risks you've already
identified, the expected value calculation may not justify the weight you're
giving it.

**What this means:** This isn't advice to leave. It's a prompt to honestly
evaluate: what would have to be true for the equity to be worth the current
concentration of effort and income dependency? Write down the assumptions.
Check them.

---

### 7. Your kids are watching you build

Pimpi exists because you're a parent. TLOM exists because you care about
what kids learn. Your principle says "family is the architecture, not the
constraint."

But the same soul files describe a father running 55 sessions per day,
managing five ventures, and operating at cognitive overload. Kids don't see
your portfolio — they see whether you're present.

This isn't a guilt trip. It's an observation that the person who built "no
guilt mechanics" and "the app is there when they show up, it doesn't guilt
them when they don't" should apply the same compassion to himself.

---

### 8. Your cultural bridge is under-leveraged commercially

Mexican roots, Belgian career, Dutch residence, English tech world. Three
languages plus one in progress. Products designed for EMEA and LATAM markets.
A genuine understanding of how education, family life, and business work
across cultures.

Most Dutch tech founders sell to the Netherlands. Most EdTech founders sell
to one market. You have authentic access to at least three markets and the
cultural fluency to build for them. This is barely showing up in your
revenue strategy.

---

### 9. You know your patterns but don't always follow them

"Protect energy, not just time" — but you run 55-session days.
"Depth over breadth" — but you're running five ventures.
"Ship over spec" — but some ventures have been in build mode for months
without a paying customer.
"Recommend, don't present menus" — but you sometimes present yourself with
too many options about what to work on next.

The principles are right. The gap is between knowing and doing. Every AI
you work with sees this gap — that's why they all independently encode
"help him prioritize ruthlessly" as a core behavior.

## What to do with this

This isn't a to-do list. It's a mirror.

Read it when you're about to start something new and ask: *should I be
finishing something instead?*

Read it when you're grinding at midnight and ask: *is this protecting my
energy or burning it?*

Read it when someone asks about your equity and ask: *have I actually
checked those assumptions recently?*

Read it quarterly alongside the principles. Update it when something
changes materially. Delete entries that no longer apply.
